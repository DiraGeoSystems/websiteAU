"Hic Sunt Dracones" or "Here Be Dragons" was a phrase used by medieval cartographers to mark dangerous or unexplored territories on maps. While the exact Latin phrase appears on only one known historical artifact—the Hunt-Lenox Globe (c. 1510)—sea monsters and mythical creatures were commonly drawn on uncharted areas of medieval maps, warning sailors of the unknown dangers that might await them.
